#Painting

Interior decoration means determine the look and feel of a home.Interior decorators help determine the look and feel of a client's home by selecting and placing decorative elements such as paint, textiles, and furniture. These interior design elements include light, colour, texture and pattern,shape.

Adding so many different textures helps make the room feel more expensive and expensive, so don’t be afraid to throw lots of decorative pillows, knitting, blankets and thick, luxurious drains - it will further enhance the feel of the space. Principles of design are simple guidelines used to combine different elements of design light, texture, color, etc. into a design.

Use mirrors to balance certain color combinations when adding a look to any modern living room. Instead of adding too much art, use a larger piece that becomes the main focal point. Insert a pillow mix into the design to draw attention to a naturally simple bed.

8 Best Office Design Ideas & Trends

1. Mix several cans of paint in a large bucket for a consistent color throughout the room

The color of the paint may vary slightly from one to another. If you want to open a new can in the middle of the wall, you will notice the difference. Mixing the paint together eliminates the problem. It is best to estimate the amount of paint you need and mix it into the bucket.
Here are some great painting tips:
When coverage is hard to predict, add more or less when painting a DIY wall. You can always pour in the leftovers. For larger jobs, use buckets and roller screens instead of roller trays. Loading your roller on the screen is much faster than using a roller pan. Just dip the roller into the paint bucket, then roll it on the screen until it stops dripping

2. Let the paint dry, then cut the tape loose for a perfect edge

Once the paint is dry you can’t just remove the tape from the trim. Removing pieces of tape from the dried paint through the wall is very laborious. Before pulling the tape, cut it. Wait until the paint is completely dry, at least 24 hours. Then use a knife or box cutter knife to cut from the border. Start in an inconsistent area to make sure the paint is hard enough to clean. If you cut the paint while it is still tasty, you will mess up. As soon as you cut the paint, pull the tape at a 45-degree angle.

3. Paint the trim first, then the ceiling and walls

They usually follow a certain order when painting a room. They paint the trim first, then the ceiling, then the walls. Because it is easier and faster to cut the trim than to cut the walls. And you definitely don’t want to tape these two! When painting the trim, you don’t have to be neat. Just focus on the smooth finish on the wood.Don't worry if the trim paint gets on the walls. When painting the walls you cover it later. Once the trim is completely painted and dry at least 24 hours, tape it, then the ceiling, then paint the walls.

4. Prime and texture wall patches to avoid a blotchy finish

Freshly painted walls often look black. The color is the same, but the sheen is not consistent. It usually occurs on pores and cracks you have filled with filler or drywall compound. Porous fillers absorb paint by reducing the surface area. When the light hits these dull spots, they stick out like the thumbs of a throat. The smooth patch is also against the slightly raised structure of the rest of the wall.2. A quick coat of primer - takes everything to eliminate ash and texture differences. The primer seals the patch so that the paint does not sink and does not look dull. To match the texture, prime with a roller, assembling the edges out. To match the structure of the surrounding walls choose the thickness of the nap.

5. Clean dirty surfaces so the paint can form a strong bond

If you paint on dirty, oily surfaces, the paint will easily chip or peel off. So before painting, clean minor parts with a degloser or heavy-duty cleaner for the purpose of prepaint cleaning. They work well for cleaning painted, varnished or enameled surfaces to improve the viscosity of new paints.They are ideal for lubricating or oily areas like kitchen and bathroom walls and for removing hand marks around light switches and dockernobs.Wipe the cleaner in a circular motion using a lint-free cloth or abrasive pad. Start at the bottom and work. Once the surfaces are clean, fill in any nicks and holes, then sand them smoothly before painting. Cleaners are available at paint stores and home centers. Wear rubber gloves and eye protection.

6. Roll paint along the edges for consistent texture

Only the corners painted with the brush and the front of the trim have a significantly different texture than the paint around them. To make sure the finished texture is consistent in this area, brush on the paint, then roll it immediately before the paint dries. Use a 3-inch roller with a nodule as thick as the roller used for the rest of the wall. Roll as close as you can without throwing stones on the reverse wall or sliding the paint on the trim. Finish brushing on paint and wrapping it in one area before moving on to the next section.

7. Use cotton drop cloths rather than plastic

The thick canvas stays in place, so don't need to tape it. Plastic drop clothes are slippery to move forward or set the ladder and cannot stay in place. Worst of all, paint spills on plastic stay wet and they can go on your shoes and track the house. The canvas is slippery on hard-ores, so rosin paper is better than vinyl, tile and hard wood. Tap the sheets together and floor to provide a nonslip surface. But even with canvas or rosin-paper drop cloths, large leaks must be wiped off immediately or they will penetrate. Clean leaks with paper towels or rags. Similarly, if you spray paint on any other surface, wipe it off immediately.
