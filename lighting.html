#Lighting Decoration

Lighting decoration means determine the look and feel of a home.Interior decorators help determine the look and feel of a client's home by selecting and placing decorative elements such as paint, textiles, and furniture. This client-facing role involves meeting directly with clients to get a sense of how they intend to use their space and how they want it to feel. These interior design elements include light, colour, texture and pattern,shape,form; and keeping them balanced is the key to creating an aesthetically pleasing interior.

Adding lots of different textures helps to make a room look more expensive and feel, cozier, so don't be afraid to throw in lots of decorative pillows, chunky knit throws, blankets, and thick, luxurious drapes â€“ it will make the space feel more like home and add an extra layer of style. The principles of design are simple guidelines used to organize the various elements of design (light,texture, color, etc.) in a composition.

Use mirrors to balance a fixed color scheme while adding a polished look to any modern living room. Instead of adding too much art, stick with one large piece that becomes the main focal point. Add an eclectic mix of pillows to draw attention to a couch that is naturally simple in design.

8 Best Office Design Ideas & Trends

1. Make a lighting plan

If you're starting from scratch or redecorating a room, create a mini brief or lighting plan that tackles the essentials. What activities take place in each room like eating, relaxing, working, key features of a room you want to highlight and what architectural boundaries you may need to take into account. Consider colour temperature,style, scale, output and even of lights before you go rushing into design decisions. Early planning makes for less headaches and rushed last minute decisions.

2. Layer your lighting

Take a layered approach to lighting with different light sources across different levels to create ambience and interest in a room. Use lighting to make the most of a room's size and shape.Uplighting makes a room feel larger and clusters of lighting make large rooms seem cozier.

3. Make sure the lighting is helpful

Consider what tasks you may be undertaking in each room where lighting can affect or aid you. Cooking requires more focused lighting, therefore, a combination of bright downlights and recessed lighting, in cabinets and stove tops, is useful. Flexible and directional lighting is better to keep you away from reading. Powder rooms require a combination of sidelights and downlights. Dimmers will quickly become your new best friend, providing an energy efficient and effective way to quickly change the atmosphere and heat of a room.

4. Choose the bulb carefully

The bulbs you choose should be more than an afterthought, racing through the supermarket when you realise they've blown.

5. Use spotlights to highlight your favourites

Use lighting to subtly (or not so subtly) focus on a feature wall, architectural element or key possession (like your mahogany leather sofa or winged armchair). Symmetrically arranged walled walls on either side of a statement mirror above the fireplace's mantlepiece will add warmth, sophistication and elegance to a room.

6. Make your guests feel welcome

Spotlights work well pointed at the centre of a dining table to draw people in.

7. Light the way at night

The midnight bathroom break is made worse with harsh lighting that causes temporary blindness when the switch is flaunted. Invest in directional floor level lighting in hallways, stairs and bathrooms to create subtle warmth and a guide.

8. Less is more

Keep themes, color schemes, and content consistent throughout the house, especially when it comes to lighting. Many different styles in a different array in home fill can be confusing to the eye and will cause discomfort. Neutral, lighter tones will open up a space and make it appear larger, adding interest to the color receptacles (such as through lampsheds), while darker colors are a tactile version of luxury and cosmic. Keep it simple and well-organized.
