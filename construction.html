#2. Tips for House Construction

All Construction Projects use these four major phases:
1. Concept Phase
2. Design and tendering Phase
3. Execution Phase
4.Finishing Phase
1. Concept Phase

DESIGN

  • Select the appointed an Architect, give your requirements
  • The architect shall present various concepts for your approval
  • Finalise the architectural design
  • Based on the finalised architectural design consultants shall develop their design
  • Appoint interior designer, if need be
PROJECT BUDGET
  • Based on the final design space requirement and the finishes budget.

2. Design and tendering Phase

Detail design

  • Based on the Architectural drawings the the plumbing ,electrical consultants and structural engineer develop on the design and details.
  • This is the stage when one decides as to what flooring material one wishes to use. For example: is it marble floor, vitrified tiles, wooden floor etc., Similarly one has to decide on the type of ceramic fixtures like WC, washbasin, and fittings (Taps).
  • Decisions have to be taken on your requirement of light points, power points, TV cable outlets, Internet, telephone outlets, ceiling fans, Air-conditioning, geyser, etc., These details are required at the total power requirements shown on the Electrical Construction drawings.

Statutory Approvals from concerned authorities
  • Onec you have finalised on the concept drawings, make sure that the design meets all the statutory requirements.
  • Obtain statutory sanctions by submitting the drawings for approval to the authorities.
  • Obtain sufficient copies of these sanction drawings, as these will required to be submitted to the authorities for power, water and sewage connections.
Tender phase
  • This phase when you get the maximum amount of unsolicited advise.
  • The Architects and the consultants shall submit a cost estimate based on the Bill prepared by them.
  • The estimate shall be as accurate as possible, considering that all the requirements are finalised.
  • The tender document shall be issued to the pre-qualified contractors to submit their bids.
  • The contractors are invited for negotiations. This meeting helps is not only in understanding each other but also to clarify all doubts both the parties may possess.
3. Construction Phase
  • This is the phase when three aspects of the project are Time, Cost and Quality.
  • Contractor mobilises his infrastructure to commence work.
  • Construction drawings for commencement of work must be made available immediately.
  • Architects and other consultants shall submit a schedule for release of drawings.
  • This will facilitate the contractors to plan his activities.
Quality Management
  • Quality of materials used and the workmanship shall be checked.
  • Quality of materials shall be checked as per the IS code requirements.
  • Quality of work has to be checked by an engineer during construction.
  • A set of checklists may be used to check the quality of work being executed.
4. Finishing phase
  • All the documents that are connected with the project, such as Structural,Architectural and services drawings, final bills of various vendors, warranties and guarantees are collected.
  • Ensure that all statutory drawings such as Building sanction drawings are filed safe and secure.
